Coupling 101

Coupling is the degree of interdependence between software modules; a measure of how closely connected two routines or modules are; the strength of the relationships between modules.

Wikipedia

Cohesion refers to the degree to which the elements inside a module belong together. Thus, cohesion measures the strength of relationship between pieces of functionality within a given module.

Wikipedia

Connascence - two components are connascent if a change in one would require the other to be modified in order to maintain the overall correctness of the system.

Wikipedia

COUPLING VS COHESION

  • Coupling: interdependencies between "modules"

  • Cohesion: how related the functions within a single "module" are
